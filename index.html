Pillars to a Healthy Lifestyle

Introduction to the Four Pillars

With the right support system in place, adopting a healthier family lifestyle can be achievable for both children and parents. At Johns Hopkins All Children’s Hospital, we believe healthy lives are built on four pillars of support: Diet, Exercise, Sleep, and Behavioral Health.

  1. Diet: It can be a challenge to eat right when surrounded by processed, high-calorie foods, high-sugar beverages, and super-size portions. It is no surprise that more children battle weight gain, obesity, and other health issues. Yet, adopting healthier eating habits can help, such as choosing more whole foods and healthy snacks and learning how to manage meal sizes.
  2. Exercise: An active body is a healthy body. Regular exercise can help manage weight, boost mood, and improve sleep. Guidelines suggest at least 60 minutes per day of moderate-to-vigorous activity for optimal health. Activities that children enjoy makes it easier for them to get moving and stay moving.
  3. Sleep: Without adequate sleep, people are more likely to overeat and exercise less. They also have a higher risk of depression and have trouble with concentration. For children, poor sleep also can affect academic performance. Following lifestyle habits, such as watching caffeine intake, managing electronic screen time, and keeping a regular sleep schedule, can help everyone get the sleep they need.
  4. Behavioral Health: Mood and mental health is an often overlooked but crucial part of overall health. Understanding how to manage children’s anxieties, fears, and other emotions provide the additional support they need to maintain a healthy diet, proper exercise, and adequate sleep.
